15 Best Weekend Trips from Mumbai (2026) - Beaches, Hills, and Forts

By WIB TeamUpdated March 2026

Mumbai is perfectly positioned for weekend getaways - hills of the Western Ghats to the east, Konkan coastline to the south, and wine country to the north. Here are 15 trips organized by distance.

Under 100 km (day trip possible)

1. Lonavala and Khandala - 83 km (2 hrs)

The most popular getaway from Mumbai and Pune. During monsoon (Jun-Sep), every cliff has a waterfall. Tiger Point, Bhushi Dam, Rajmachi Fort trek. The Expressway drive is part of the experience. Budget: Rs 2,000-6,000 for a day/overnight. Stay: The Machan (luxury treehouse, Rs 15,000), Della Resorts (mid-range, Rs 5,000), MTDC Resort (budget, Rs 1,200).

2. Matheran - 80 km (2.5 hrs)

India's only automobile-free hill station. You park at Dasturi Naka and walk or take a horse. Toy train from Neral is a delightful ride. Charlotte Lake, Panorama Point, Echo Point. The peace and quiet is the main attraction. Budget: Rs 2,000-5,000. Best from October to May.

3. Alibaug - 95 km (2 hrs by road / 1 hr by ferry)

Take the Mandwa ferry from Gateway of India for a scenic 1-hour ride. Alibaug Beach, Kolaba Fort (walk to it at low tide), Kashid Beach (30 km further for better sand). Budget: Rs 2,500-6,000. Stay: Radisson Blu (Rs 6,000), U Tropicana (Rs 3,500).

100-200 km (overnight recommended)

4. Igatpuri - 120 km (2.5 hrs)

Home to the Vipassana meditation centre. Tringalwadi Fort trek, Vaitarna Dam, Bhandardara (nearby). Cooler than Mumbai year-round. Budget: Rs 1,500-4,000. A peaceful alternative to crowded Lonavala.

5. Kashid Beach - 130 km (3 hrs)

White sand beach on the Konkan coast. Much less crowded than Alibaug. The drive along the coastal road through Murud is scenic. Budget: Rs 2,000-5,000. Best October to May.

6. Nashik - 170 km (3.5 hrs)

Wine capital of India. Visit Sula Vineyards, York Winery, Soma for wine tasting. Trimbakeshwar Temple (one of 12 Jyotirlingas). Panchvati ghats. The food scene is excellent. Budget: Rs 3,000-8,000. Stay: Beyond by Sula (Rs 8,000), Ginger (Rs 2,500).

200-350 km (weekend trip)

7. Mahabaleshwar and Panchgani - 260 km (5 hrs)

Twin hill stations with strawberry farms, Table Land (Asia's second-longest mountain plateau), Arthur Seat point, Mapro Garden. Best November to May. Budget: Rs 4,000-10,000. Stay: Brightland Resort (Rs 5,000), Le Meridien (Rs 8,000).

8. Kolhapur - 380 km (6 hrs)

Mahalakshmi Temple, Kolhapuri cuisine (the misal pav here is legendary), Panhala Fort, New Palace Museum. A food lover's paradise. Budget: Rs 3,000-6,000. Train from Mumbai CST is convenient (overnight).

Frequently asked questions

What are the best weekend trips from Mumbai? +
Top weekend trips from Mumbai: Lonavala (83 km, 2 hrs), Alibaug (95 km, 2 hrs or ferry), Mahabaleshwar (260 km, 5 hrs), Matheran (80 km, 2.5 hrs - no vehicles allowed), Nashik (170 km, 3.5 hrs), Igatpuri (120 km, 2.5 hrs), Panchgani (245 km, 5 hrs), and Kashid Beach (130 km, 3 hrs).
Which is the nearest beach from Mumbai for a weekend? +
Alibaug is the nearest beach destination at 95 km (2 hrs by road or 1 hr by Mandwa ferry from Gateway of India). Kashid Beach (130 km) is more secluded and scenic. For a proper beach holiday, Tarkarli (480 km, 8 hrs) has the clearest water on the Konkan coast.
