15 Best Weekend Trips from Delhi (2026) - Mountains, Lakes, and Heritage

By WIB TeamUpdated March 2026

Delhi NCR residents have mountain getaways, heritage cities, tiger reserves, and spiritual destinations all within a 6-hour drive. Here are 15 trips that work perfectly for a 2-day break.

Under 150 km (day trip possible)

1. Neemrana Fort - 120 km (2.5 hrs)

A 15th-century fort converted into a heritage hotel. Even if you don't stay overnight, the zip-lining across the fort walls is thrilling. Sunday brunch at the fort is popular. Budget: Rs 3,000-15,000 (day visit to luxury stay).

2. Sultanpur Bird Sanctuary - 50 km (1.5 hrs)

Perfect for a morning trip. Over 250 bird species. Best during winter (Nov-Feb) when migratory birds visit. Entry: Rs 40. Ideal for families with children interested in nature.

150-250 km (overnight recommended)

3. Mathura-Vrindavan - 160 km (3 hrs)

Birthplace of Lord Krishna. Prem Mandir (illuminated at night), Banke Bihari Temple, Iskcon, boat ride on Yamuna. During Holi (March), Vrindavan celebrations are legendary. Budget: Rs 2,000-5,000.

4. Agra - 230 km (3.5 hrs)

Taj Mahal at sunrise is a must-see at least once. Agra Fort, Fatehpur Sikri, Mehtab Bagh for sunset Taj view. Yamuna Expressway makes the drive smooth. Budget: Rs 3,000-8,000. Gatimaan Express train is also quick (1.5 hrs).

5. Rishikesh - 240 km (5.5 hrs)

Rafting, bungee jumping, Beatles Ashram, Lakshman Jhula, Triveni Ghat aarti. The ultimate adventure + spiritual combo. Budget: Rs 3,000-8,000. Camp stay at Shivpuri for the full experience.

250-350 km (weekend trip)

6. Jaipur - 280 km (4.5 hrs)

Pink City. Amber Fort, Hawa Mahal, City Palace, Nahargarh Fort sunset, Albert Hall Museum. The street food (dal baati churma, pyaaz kachori, lassi) is incredible. Budget: Rs 4,000-10,000. Shatabdi train: 4.5 hrs.

7. Mussoorie - 290 km (6 hrs)

Queen of Hills. Mall Road, Kempty Falls, Gun Hill cable car, Lal Tibba viewpoint. Less crowded than Shimla. Budget: Rs 4,000-10,000. November-February for snow views.

8. Jim Corbett National Park - 260 km (5.5 hrs)

India's oldest national park. Tiger safari, elephant rides, jeep drives through sal forests. Bijrani and Dhikala zones are best for tiger sightings. Budget: Rs 5,000-15,000 including safari. Best November to June.

Frequently asked questions

What are the best weekend trips from Delhi? +
Top weekend trips from Delhi: Agra (230 km, 3.5 hrs), Jaipur (280 km, 4.5 hrs), Rishikesh (240 km, 5.5 hrs), Mussoorie (290 km, 6 hrs), Jim Corbett (260 km, 5.5 hrs), Neemrana (120 km, 2.5 hrs), Mathura-Vrindavan (160 km, 3 hrs), and Nainital (300 km, 6.5 hrs).
Which is the nearest hill station from Delhi? +
Mussoorie (290 km, 6 hrs) and Lansdowne (250 km, 5.5 hrs) are the nearest proper hill stations. For a shorter drive, Morni Hills in Haryana (260 km, 4.5 hrs) offers a quick hill escape. Shimla (340 km, 7 hrs) is further but has better infrastructure.
