For cardiac surgery: Madras Medical Mission

Founded by the legendary Dr. K.M. Cherian who performed India's first coronary bypass. The hospital specializes exclusively in cardiac and vascular surgery with outcomes matching international standards at Indian prices.

For eye care: Sankara Nethralaya

Rated 4.8, this is arguably the best eye hospital in India. Founded in 1978, it handles everything from routine cataract to complex retinal surgeries. The Jaslok Kamalnayan Bajaj Institute within the campus is a research powerhouse.

For orthopedics: MIOT International

India's largest joint replacement center. They perform over 3,000 joint replacements annually. Their sports medicine department also treats IPL cricketers and ISL footballers.

Frequently asked questions

Which is the best hospital in Chennai? +
Apollo Hospitals Greams Road is considered the best overall hospital in Chennai with JCI and NABH accreditation, 710 beds, and excellence across cardiac, oncology, transplant, and neurosciences. For specific needs: Sankara Nethralaya for eyes, MIOT for orthopedics, and Madras Medical Mission for cardiac surgery.
Which is the best government hospital in Chennai? +
Rajiv Gandhi Government General Hospital (GGH) is the largest with 2,400 beds. Stanley Medical College Hospital is well-regarded for surgery and trauma. Both accept Ayushman Bharat and provide free treatment for eligible patients.
Which hospital in Chennai is best for cancer treatment? +
Apollo Hospitals and Adyar Cancer Institute (Cancer Institute WIA) are the top choices for cancer treatment in Chennai. Adyar Cancer Institute is a dedicated oncology center with decades of experience and is more affordable than private hospitals.
Do Chennai hospitals accept all health insurance? +
Most private hospitals in Chennai accept major TPA health insurance (Star Health, ICICI Lombard, New India, etc). Government hospitals accept CGHS, ECHS, and Ayushman Bharat. Always verify your specific policy and hospital empanelment before admission.
