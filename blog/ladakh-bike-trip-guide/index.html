Ladakh Bike Trip Guide (2026) - Complete Route, Budget, and Tips

By WIB TeamUpdated March 202610 min read

The Manali to Leh bike trip is every Indian rider's dream. 475 km of the most dramatic road on Earth, crossing 5 high passes above 4,000 meters.

10-day itinerary

Day 1: Arrive Manali, acclimatize

Reach by overnight bus from Delhi (Rs 800-1,500). Rent bike from Mall Road (Royal Enfield Himalayan: Rs 1,500-2,000/day). Rest and check bike. Do NOT ride on arrival day.

Day 2: Manali to Sissu (115 km, 5 hrs)

Cross Atal Tunnel. Easy ride through Lahaul Valley. Sissu waterfall. Alt: 3,100m.

Day 3: Sissu to Sarchu (110 km, 6 hrs)

First high pass - Baralacha La (4,890m). Drink plenty water. Sarchu is tent camp. Alt: 4,290m.

Day 4: Sarchu to Leh (250 km, 10 hrs)

Longest day. Cross Nakee La, Lachalung La, Tanglang La (5,328m). 21 Gata Loops hairpins. Reach Leh evening. Alt: 3,500m.

Day 5: Rest in Leh

Essential acclimatization. Leh Palace, Shanti Stupa, markets. Drink 3-4L water. Headache is normal.

Day 6: Leh to Nubra Valley (120 km, 5 hrs)

Cross Khardung La (5,359m). Diskit Monastery. Camel ride at Hunder sand dunes.

Day 7: Nubra to Pangong Lake (160 km, 7 hrs)

Via Shyok road. Pangong Tse at 4,350m - the blue changes shade hourly. Camp by the lake.

Day 8: Pangong to Leh (160 km, 6 hrs)

Via Chang La (5,360m). Last high pass. Back to Leh for rest.

Day 9-10: Return Leh to Manali

Same route back. 2 days riding. Return bike. Celebrate.

Essential tips

  • Carry Diamox for altitude sickness (consult doctor first)
  • Fill fuel at every pump - gaps of 100-200 km common
  • Royal Enfield Himalayan is the best bike choice - 411cc handles altitude well
  • Inner Line Permit needed for Nubra, Pangong, Tso Moriri (apply online or in Leh)
  • Never ride alone - minimum 2 bikes for safety
  • BSNL has best network coverage in Ladakh
  • Layered clothing essential - 30 degree temperature swings in one day

Frequently asked questions

How much does a Ladakh bike trip cost? +
Budget Rs 25,000-40,000 per person for 10 days. Includes bike rental (Rs 1,200-2,000/day), fuel (Rs 4,500), accommodation (Rs 500-1,500/night), food (Rs 500-800/day), and permits (Rs 400).
Which bike is best for Ladakh? +
Royal Enfield Himalayan 411cc. Handles altitude well, comfortable for long rides, widely available for rent in Manali at Rs 1,500-2,000/day. Avoid bikes below 350cc.
Best time for Ladakh bike trip? +
June to September. Roads open mid-June. July-August warmest but some rain. September has clear skies and autumn colors but very cold nights.
